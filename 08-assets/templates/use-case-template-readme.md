## üìä Template Structure Overview

The template is organized into 14 sections, grouped by use case maturity:

### üéØ Phase 1: Initial Proposal (For Prioritization)
**Required for DVF and WSJF workshops**

| # | Section | Key Content | Time |
|---|---------|-------------|------|
| 1 | Executive Summary | One-page overview, strategic alignment | 30 min |
| 2 | Business Case | Quantified benefits, costs, ROI, benchmarks | 1-2 hrs |
| 3 | Current State | Pain points, stakeholders, current process | 1 hr |
| 4 | Target State | AI solution, data, technology, patterns | 2-3 hrs |
| 5 | Implementation Plan | Timeline, dependencies, resources | 1 hr |
| 6 | Prioritization Scoring | WSJF, DVF, multi-dimensional scores | 30 min |

**Deliverable**: Use case ready for prioritization (6-8 hours)

### üéØ Phase 2: Detailed Planning (Post-Prioritization)
**Required before implementation begins**

| # | Section | Key Content | Time |
|---|---------|-------------|------|
| 7 | Risk Management | Risks, mitigations, model risk, compliance | 2 hrs |
| 8 | Success Metrics & KPIs | Business and technical KPIs, monitoring | 1 hr |
| 9 | Governance & Compliance | Governance model, audit requirements | 1 hr |
| 10 | Change Management | Training, rollout, stakeholder engagement | 2 hrs |
| 11 | Post-Implementation | Hypercare, continuous improvement, benefits tracking | 1 hr |

**Deliverable**: Detailed use case ready for implementation (7 hours)

### üéØ Phase 3: Documentation & Approvals
**Final documentation and sign-offs**

| # | Section | Key Content | Time |
|---|---------|-------------|------|
| 12 | References & Appendices | Links, glossary, related documents | 30 min |

## üí° Top Tips for Success

### 1. Don't Start from Scratch
- Review existing use case examples (UC-001, UC-004, UC-009)
- Reference architecture patterns (patterns/ directory)
- Use comparable examples from industry

### 2. Quantify Everything
- ‚ùå "Improve efficiency" 
- ‚úÖ "Reduce processing time by 60% (5 days ‚Üí 2 days), saving $800K annually"

### 3. Get Help Early
- Enterprise Architect: Technical architecture, patterns
- Risk & Compliance: Model risk, governance
- Change Manager: Stakeholder engagement, training

### 4. Use the Right Level of Detail
- **For prioritization**: Sections 1-6 are sufficient
- **For implementation**: Complete Sections 1-11
- **Don't over-engineer**: Focus on what's needed now

### 5. Follow the Quality Checklist
- Before submitting, check Section 14 of the template
- Ensure all required sections complete
- Verify benefits are quantified
- Confirm patterns are referenced

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Common Pitfalls to Avoid

1. **Vague Benefits**: Quantify with $ values or % improvements
2. **Technology-First**: Start with business problem, not tech solution
3. **Unrealistic Timelines**: Include discovery, pilot, rollout (not just build)
4. **Missing Change Management**: Allocate 10-15% of budget
5. **No Governance Plan**: Include from day 1, not afterthought
6. **No Comparable Examples**: Build credibility with industry benchmarks

See full list in **Guide Section: Common Pitfalls to Avoid**

## üìà What Happens After You Submit?

### New Use Cases (Prioritization)
1. Add to prioritization spreadsheet (Sheet 1)
2. Participate in DVF workshop ‚Üí Get Pass/Fail
3. If Pass, participate in WSJF workshop ‚Üí Get WSJF score
4. Dependency mapping ‚Üí Get wave assignment
5. Portfolio review ‚Üí Possible inclusion in Top 10/Top 5
6. If approved, proceed to detailed planning

### Approved Use Cases (Implementation)
1. Complete detailed planning (Sections 7-11)
2. Create architecture and sequence diagrams
3. Submit to TAF (Technical Architecture Forum)
4. Obtain approvals (Section 13)
5. Commence delivery once approved

## üóÇÔ∏è File Naming Convention

Use this naming convention for your use case document:

```
UC-[Number]-[UseCase-Name]-v[Version].md
```

**Examples**:
- `UC-025-Customer-Insights-v1.0.0.md`
- `UC-026-Risk-Analytics-v1.0.0.md`
- `UC-027-Procurement-Automation-v1.0.0.md`

**Version Numbers**:
- `v0.1`: Initial draft
- `v1.0.0`: First approved version
- `v1.1.0`: Minor updates (no re-approval needed)
- `v2.0.0`: Major changes (requires re-approval)
