**üö™ ONE-WAY DOORS (Decide Now - High Urgency):**
- **ISV Agent Reusability**: Platform architecture determines future flexibility
- **Platform Modularity**: Core architectural decisions create long-term lock-in

- **Target Users**: Democratization strategies can evolve
- **Operations Strategy**: Can transition from vendor-managed to self-managed
- **Update Cadence**: Governance processes can be refined

### SPEAKER NOTES:
Reference: Strategic Questions Overview section, Bezos' Door Framework classification

Using Bezos' Door Framework from Amazon's decision-making philosophy, we've classified the six strategic questions by reversibility. One-way doors require careful upfront consideration because they're difficult or costly to reverse. Platform architecture and vendor ecosystem decisions fall into this category - once established, changing becomes expensive and complex. Two-way doors can be approached iteratively, allowing learning and adjustment over time. This framework helps prioritize where to invest decision-making resources immediately versus where experimentation is acceptable.

## Slide 8: Risk Mitigation Strategy
### SLIDE CONTENT:
**Addressing Partnership Risks Through Strategic Design**

**Vendor Dependency Risks:**
- Multi-vendor approach across different technology layers
- Data portability rights in all contracts
- Open standards where possible

**Capability Development:**
- Build internal AI literacy without requiring platform development
- Maintain strategic control while leveraging partner execution
- Focus on governance leadership and vendor management

**Exit Strategy Planning:**
- Component modularity enables incremental transitions
- Skills development supports future optionality

### SPEAKER NOTES:
Reference: Risk Mitigation Strategy section under Learning from Comparable-Scale Banks

Acknowledging partnership risks is crucial for stakeholder confidence. The modular architecture approach enables vendor diversification - different providers for different layers reduces single-vendor dependency. The governance leadership approach means BNZ maintains strategic decision-making while leveraging Accenture's execution capabilities. This balanced approach follows successful patterns from risk-conscious banks in regulated environments.
