## Risk Assessment & Mitigation Strategies

### High-Risk Considerations

#### 1. Regulatory Compliance Risk
**Risk**: AI systems may inadvertently violate banking regulations or data privacy laws
**Impact**: Potential fines ($1M-$10M), regulatory scrutiny, reputational damage
**Mitigation**:
- Dedicated compliance workstream with legal and risk teams
- Regular regulatory body engagement (RBNZ, FMA)
- Comprehensive audit trails and explainability frameworks
- Gradual rollout with extensive testing

#### 2. Technology Vendor Lock-in
**Risk**: Over-dependence on AWS ecosystem limiting future flexibility
**Impact**: Increased costs, reduced negotiating power, migration complexity
**Mitigation**:
- Multi-cloud strategy development
- Open standards adoption where possible
- Regular vendor relationship reviews
- Exit strategy planning

#### 3. Skills and Talent Gap
**Risk**: Insufficient internal capability to execute effectively
**Impact**: Project delays, quality issues, competitive disadvantage
**Mitigation**:
- Aggressive training and certification programs
- Strategic hiring of AI expertise
- External partnership and consulting support
- Knowledge sharing and mentorship programs

### Medium-Risk Considerations

#### 4. Customer Trust and Acceptance
**Risk**: Customers may resist AI-driven services or lose trust
**Impact**: Reduced adoption, negative publicity, customer churn
**Mitigation**:
- Transparent AI communication strategy
- Gradual introduction with human oversight
- Strong privacy and security messaging
- Customer feedback loops and adaptation

#### 5. Integration Complexity
**Risk**: Difficulty integrating with legacy banking systems
**Impact**: Extended timelines, cost overruns, system instability
**Mitigation**:
- Comprehensive integration testing
- Phased rollout approach
- Legacy system modernization planning
- Robust fallback procedures
