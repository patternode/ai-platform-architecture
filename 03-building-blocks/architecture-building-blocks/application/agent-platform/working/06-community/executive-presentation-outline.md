# Executive Presentation: AWS agentCore & Agentic AI Community of Interest

## Presentation Structure (45-60 minutes)

### Opening (5 minutes)
**Slide 1: Title & Context**
- Community of Interest Proposal: AWS agentCore & Agentic AI
- Seeking executive sponsorship from both GMs
- Strategic investment in BNZ's AI future

**Slide 2: Agenda**
- Strategic rationale and market context
- Community scope and structure
- Investment requirements and ROI projections
- Risk assessment and mitigation
- Sponsor commitments and next steps

### Strategic Context (10 minutes)

**Slide 3: The Agentic AI Opportunity**
- Market size: $28.5B by 2028, banking fastest-growing sector
- Competitive threat: Major banks already deploying at scale
- BNZ opportunity: First-mover advantage in New Zealand market

**Slide 4: Industry Examples**
- Commonwealth Bank "Ceba": 2M+ customers, 30% cost reduction
- JPMorgan IndexGPT: $5T assets under AI-enhanced management
- Wells Fargo predictive banking: 15% churn reduction

**Slide 5: BNZ Strategic Alignment**
- Digital First Strategy: AI-native customer experiences
- Operational Excellence: Intelligent automation
- Innovation Leadership: Market differentiation
- Risk Management: Enhanced compliance and fraud detection

### Community Proposal (15 minutes)

**Slide 6: Community Mission & Vision**
- Mission: Establish BNZ as agentic AI leader through collaboration
- Vision: Interconnected AI ecosystem enhancing all customer touchpoints
- Focus: AWS agentCore platform and enterprise-grade implementation

**Slide 7: Governance Structure**
- Executive Sponsorship: Both GMs + CTO advisory
- Community Leadership: Solution Architect + Principal Engineer
- Working Groups: Platform, Development, Use Cases, Governance & Risk
- Membership: 15-20 core, 50-100 extended community

**Slide 8: Key Activities & Deliverables**
- Monthly community meetings and bi-weekly working groups
- Quarterly innovation workshops and annual conference
- Technical standards, training programs, pilot implementations
- 2026 roadmap: 4 quarters of progressive capability building

**Slide 9: Success Metrics**
- Technical: Agent performance, development velocity, platform adoption
- Business: Customer satisfaction, operational efficiency, innovation rate
- Community: Engagement, knowledge growth, external recognition

### Investment & ROI (10 minutes)

**Slide 10: Investment Requirements**
- Year 1 Total: $1.6M - $2.4M
- Technology: $600K-$900K (AWS services, tools, infrastructure)
- People: $800K-$1.2M (leadership, community time, training)
- Training: $200K-$300K (certifications, conferences, development)

**Slide 11: ROI Projections**
- 3-Year ROI: 250-400%
- Cost Savings: $3.5M-$5.5M annually (automation, efficiency)
- Revenue Enhancement: $5M-$9M annually (personalization, new services)
- Break-even: 18-24 months

**Slide 12: Budget Breakdown by Quarter**
- Q1: $400K-$600K (foundation, team, initial infrastructure)
- Q2: $300K-$500K (pilot development, training expansion)
- Q3: $400K-$600K (production deployment, scaling)
- Q4: $500K-$700K (enterprise rollout, advanced capabilities)

### Risk Management (10 minutes)

**Slide 13: High-Risk Considerations**
- Regulatory compliance: Mitigation through dedicated compliance workstream
- Vendor lock-in: Multi-cloud strategy and open standards adoption
- Skills gap: Aggressive training and strategic hiring programs
- Customer trust: Transparent communication and gradual introduction

**Slide 14: Risk Mitigation Strategies**
- Regulatory: RBNZ/FMA engagement, audit trails, explainability
- Technical: Integration testing, legacy modernization, fallback procedures
- Organizational: Change management, cultural transformation support
- Market: Competitive analysis, customer feedback loops, agile adaptation

**Slide 15: Success Factors**
- Executive commitment through challenges and organizational changes
- Cultural change management and AI literacy development
- Technical excellence through talent and continuous learning
- Customer-centric design with measurable satisfaction improvements

## Discussion Points for Sponsors

### Strategic Questions
1. **Market Timing**: Are we moving fast enough relative to competitors?
2. **Investment Scale**: Is the proposed investment sufficient for leadership position?
3. **Risk Appetite**: What's our tolerance for AI-related regulatory and operational risks?
4. **Success Definition**: How will we measure and communicate success?

### Operational Questions
1. **Resource Allocation**: Can we commit the required leadership and community time?
2. **Cross-Division Coordination**: How will we ensure effective collaboration?
3. **Change Management**: What support is needed for organizational adoption?
4. **Vendor Relationships**: How do we optimize our AWS partnership?

### Governance Questions
1. **Decision Authority**: What decisions require sponsor approval vs. delegation?
2. **Escalation Paths**: How do we handle risks and issues efficiently?
3. **Performance Management**: What are the quarterly review expectations?
4. **Course Correction**: How do we adapt if assumptions prove incorrect?

## Post-Presentation Actions

### If Approved
1. **Immediate** (Week 1):
   - Draft formal sponsor MOU
   - Begin community leader recruitment
   - Initiate AWS strategic partnership discussions

2. **Short-term** (Weeks 2-4):
   - Finalize governance framework
   - Secure initial budget allocation
   - Establish working group structure

3. **Foundation Phase** (Weeks 5-12):
   - Launch community activities
   - Begin first pilot development
   - Implement measurement framework

### If Deferred
1. **Reduced Scope Option**: Focus on single high-value use case
2. **Timeline Extension**: Spread investment over longer period
3. **Partnership Model**: Explore external partnership to reduce risk

### If Declined
1. **Alternative Approaches**: Smaller-scale experimentation
2. **Competitive Analysis**: Monitor market developments
3. **Future Readiness**: Maintain skill development and vendor relationships
